Egypt

Trademark Registration

Requirements:

  • The applicant's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • Notarized and Legalized Power of Attorney up to the Egyptian Consulate in the domicile of the applicant.
  • The copy of the trademark
  • The list of the goods and/or services to be covered by the application.
  • The priority document if any. (certified copy)
  • Classes and specification of the goods.
  • Multiple Class Registration is available.
  • Extra fees for color/design mark.
Time for a trademark registration: 10~11 Months

Note:

  • Multiple Class Registration is available in Egypt
  • Home/Foreign Registration not required
  • No extra Fees for Long Goods/Services

Patent Registration

Requirements:

  • The applicant's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • Notarized and legalized Power of attorney up to the Iraqi Consulate in the domicile of the applicant
  • Notarized and Legalized Power of Attorney up to the Egyptian Consulate in the domicile of the applicant.
  • Notarized and Legalized Deed of Assignment up to the Egyptian Consulate in the domicile of the applicant. (if the applicant is not the inventor)
  • Certified Priority documents (if priority is claimed)
  • Notarized and Legalized Commercial Registration Certificate up to the Egyptian Consulate in the domicile of the applicant, if the applicant is a company.
  • Specifications, Abstract and Claims along with Arabic translations

Industrial Design Registration

Requirements:

  • The applicant's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • The designer's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • Notarized and Legalized Power of Attorney up to the Egyptian Consulate in the domicile of the applicant
  • Notarized and Legalized Deed of Assignment up to the Egyptian Consulate in the domicile of the applicant. (if the applicant is not the designer)
  • Notarized and Legalized Commercial Registration Certificate up to the Egyptian Consulate in the domicile of the applicant, if the applicant is a company.
  • Four representations (photographs or drawings) of the design or model .
  • Certified Priority documents (if priority is claimed)

IP Treaties Membership:

Madrid Agreement, Madrid Protocol, Paris Convention, Patent Cooperation Treaty, Berne Convention

Trademark Registration

Patent Registration

Sudan

Trademark Registration

Requirements:

  • Information of applicant: name, address, and nationality
  • Notarized power of attorney up to the Sudanese Consulate
  • A certified and legalized copy of the Commercial Registration Certificate of the applicant
  • Classes and specification of goods/services
  • A copy of the trademark
  • A certified copy of the priority document (if claimed)
Time for Trademark Registration: 18~24 months

Note:

  • Multiple Class Registration is not available
  • Extra Fees for Color/Design Mark
  • Home/Foreign Registration is required
  • No extra Fees for Long Goods/Services

Patent Registration

Requirements:

  • Information of applicant and inventor: name, address, and nationality
  • A signed and legalized power of attorney up to the Sudanese Consulate
  • A certified and legalized copy of the certificate of incorporation of the applicant company and its articles of incorporation up to the Sudanese Consulate. A certified and legalized translation is required if the document is not in either the English language or the Arabic language
  • A copy of the patent specification and claims. An Arabic translation of the abstract of the invention only is required
  • Copy of the formal drawings
  • Copy of the priority document if priority is to be claimed.

For filing the national phase of a PCT application, the following are required:

  • PCT filing number and date
  • Particulars of the application on the basis of which priority is claimed
  • International publication number and date
  • A certified copy of the PCT application
  • A copy of the relative international publication and search report
  • A copy of PCT Preliminary Examination Report

IP Treaties Membership:

Madrid Agreement, Madrid Protocol, Paris Convention, Patent Cooperation Treaty, Berne Convention
