West Bank

Trademark Registration

Requirements:

  • The applicant's particulars: name, address and nationality.
  • Notarized and legalized power of attorney up to the West Bank Consulate.
  • Classes and specification of goods/services.
  • A copy of the trademark.
  • A Certified Copy of Priority application (if claimed).
Time for a trademark registration: 12 ~ 18 Months

IP Treaties Membership:

Paris Convention

Lebanon

Trademark Registration

Requirements:

  • The applicant's particulars: name, address, and nationality
  • Simply signed Power of Attorney, required at the time of filing.
  • Class of the goods/services.
  • Copy of the mark
  • A scanned copy Home/foreign registration certificate, if such are issued.
Time for a trademark registration: 1~2 Months

Note:

  • Multiple class registration is available in Lebanon
  • Extra Fees for Color/Design Mark
  • Extra Fees for Long Goods/Services

Patent Registration

Requirements:

  • The applicant's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • The inventor's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • Simply signed POA, required at the time of filing.
  • Notarized and Legalized Deed of Assignment up to the Lebanese Consulate in the domicile of the applicant. (if the inventor is not the applicant)
  • Certified Priority documents (if claimed)
  • Specifications, Claims, Abstract and drawings along with Arabic & French translations.

  • Simply signed POA, required at the time of filing.
  • Three copies of Specification and drawings.
  • Certified Priority documents (if priority is claimed)

Paris Convention, Berne Convention, Rome Convention

Patent Registration

Requirements:

  • The applicant's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • The inventor's particulars: name, address and nationality
  • Notarized and Legalized power of attorney up to the Qatari Consulate in the domicile of the applicant.
  • Notarized and Legalized deed of assignment up to the Qatari Consulate in the domicile of the applicant. (if the applicant is not the inventor)
  • Certified Priority documents (if priority is claimed)
  • Notarized and Legalized Commercial Registration Certificate up to the Qatari Consulate in the domicile of the applicant.
  • Arabic Translations of specifications and drawings
  • In spite of the fact that the neither the implementing regulations of Qatar Patent Law No. 30/2006, nor the prescribed official fees have been issued yet, the CPIPR will be receiving and examining the patent applications temporarily free of charge. Once the implementing regulations and associated official fees are issued by the Ministry, applicants shall be obliged to pay these fees retroactively, or else, their applications will be null and void. Nevertheless, under the present initiative of the CPIPR, a number and filing date will be assigned to all the filed applications to secure the applicants rights in their inventions.
